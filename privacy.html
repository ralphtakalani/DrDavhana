1. Introduction

Welcome to Dr. Davhana MG's practice. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal and medical information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services.

We comply with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) of South Africa and other applicable data protection laws. By using our website and services, you consent to the data practices described in this policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect various types of information to provide and improve our medical services:

Personal Information

  • Full name, date of birth, ID number
  • Residential address and contact details
  • Email address and phone numbers
  • Medical history, health conditions, and treatment records
  • Medical aid information and billing details

Website Usage Information

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent on site
  • Click patterns and navigation behavior
  • Device information and operating system

Sensitive Medical Information: As a healthcare provider, we collect sensitive health information necessary for your medical care. This information is protected under strict confidentiality agreements and POPIA regulations.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following legitimate purposes:

Medical Care

Provide diagnosis, treatment, and medical services

Appointment Management

Schedule, confirm, and remind you of appointments

Billing & Insurance

Process payments and submit medical aid claims

Quality Improvement

Analyze and improve our healthcare services

Legal Compliance

Comply with regulatory and legal requirements

Communication

Send health tips, updates, and practice news

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under POPIA, we process your personal information based on one or more of the following legal grounds:

  • Consent: You have given explicit consent for specific purposes
  • Contractual Necessity: Processing is necessary for medical treatment
  • Legal Obligation: Compliance with healthcare laws and regulations
  • Legitimate Interests: Improving healthcare services and patient care
  • Vital Interests: Protecting someone's life or health in emergencies

5. Information Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

Healthcare Providers

With specialists and other healthcare professionals involved in your care

Medical Aids

With your medical aid scheme for claim processing

Legal Authorities

When required by law or court order

Research

De-identified data for medical research (with your consent)

6. Data Security

We implement robust security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption of electronic health records
  • Secure access controls and authentication
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
  • Staff training on data protection and confidentiality
  • Secure backup and disaster recovery systems
  • Firewall and intrusion detection systems
SSL Encrypted Connection HIPAA Compliant Practices POPIA Registered

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal and medical information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide ongoing medical care and treatment
  • Comply with legal and regulatory requirements (medical records are typically retained for 6+ years)
  • Establish, exercise, or defend legal claims
  • Improve our healthcare services through analysis

After the retention period, your information will be securely destroyed or anonymized.

8. Your Rights Under POPIA

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

Right to Access

Request access to your personal information

Right to Correction

Correct inaccurate or incomplete information

Right to Deletion

Request deletion of your information (subject to legal requirements)

Right to Object

Object to processing of your information

Right to Portability

Receive your information in a portable format

Right to Complain

Lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Information Officer at privacy@drdavhana.co.za

9. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your browsing experience:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site
  • Preference Cookies: Remember your settings and preferences

You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings. However, disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

10. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites (e.g., medical aid portals, health resources). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to read their privacy policies.

11. Children's Privacy

Our services are primarily for adults, but we also treat children with parental consent. For children under 18, we collect information only with verifiable parental or guardian consent. Parents have the right to review and request deletion of their child's information.

12. International Data Transfers

Your information is primarily stored and processed within South Africa. In cases where we use international service providers (e.g., cloud services), we ensure adequate protection measures are in place compliant with POPIA requirements.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. Significant changes will be notified via email or website notice.
